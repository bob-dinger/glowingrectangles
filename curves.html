Common Curves in Nature & Society

S-Curve (Sigmoid)
Examples: Technology adoption, population growth, learning new skills, market saturation, bacterial growth in limited environment, disease spread, product lifecycle
Inverted U (Bell Curve)
Examples: Stress vs performance (Yerkes-Dodson), drug dosage effectiveness, exercise benefits, caffeine alertness, tax revenue vs rates (Laffer curve), risk vs reward
Exponential Growth
Examples: Compound interest, viral spread (early stage), Moore's Law, nuclear chain reaction, bacterial growth (unlimited), social media virality, cryptocurrency bubbles
Power Law
Examples: Wealth distribution, city populations, word frequency (Zipf), earthquake magnitude, website traffic, social media followers, book sales, citations
Logarithmic
Examples: Perceived loudness, Richter scale, human perception of differences (Weber-Fechner), pH scale, learning curve early phase, initial fitness gains
J-Curve
Examples: Private equity returns, currency devaluation effects, organizational change, new habit formation, startup trajectory, things getting worse before better
Diminishing Returns
Examples: Fertilizer on crops, hours worked vs productivity, practice after mastery, adding team members, eating more food, marginal utility of money
Hyperbolic Discounting
Examples: $100 now vs $110 next week, procrastination, addiction behavior, why we don't save for retirement, immediate gratification, impulsive decisions
Bathtub Curve
Examples: Product failure rates, human mortality, early defects → stable period → wear-out, car reliability, electronic components, machine maintenance
Long Tail
Examples: Netflix catalog, Amazon products, music streaming, niche markets, keyword searches, the 80/20 extended - endless small sellers
Step Function
Examples: Phase transitions (ice to water), tipping points, paradigm shifts, threshold effects, price tiers, viral moments, sudden regime changes
Logistic Decay
Examples: Technology obsolescence, market share loss, dying platforms, fading trends, knowledge forgetting curve, product end-of-life, declining empires
