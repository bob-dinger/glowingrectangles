Winner Takes All

One winner, many losers. Small early advantages compound until one player dominates.

This happens when:

• Network effects — more users = better product

• Switching costs — leaving is painful

• Standards — compatibility matters

Winner Takes All Examples
Google
vs AltaVista, Yahoo, Ask Jeeves
Facebook
vs MySpace, Friendster, Google+
VHS
vs Betamax
Amazon
vs Borders, Barnes & Noble
Proportional Rewards

Everyone gets a share. Better performance = bigger slice, but room for many players.

This happens when:

• Local markets — geography limits competition

• Diverse preferences — people want variety

• No lock-in — easy to switch

Proportional Examples
Restaurants
many cuisines, many price points
Coffee shops
Starbucks, local cafes, diners coexist
Law firms
many specialties, many sizes
Authors
many genres, many bestsellers
The market structure determines outcomes. Same effort, different rewards.
