Infant Moral Cognition
Are we born with a sense of right and wrong?

In 2007, researchers at Yale's Infant Cognition Center conducted a groundbreaking study on babies as young as 6 months old.

Babies watched a simple puppet show: a character tries to climb a hill. Sometimes a helper pushes it up. Sometimes a hinderer pushes it down.

When offered both characters afterward, the vast majority of babies reached for the helper.
