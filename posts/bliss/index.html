Learn Bliss

A visual language anyone can understand

Lesson 1
The Earth and Sky
Bliss Symbolics starts with the simplest ideas: the ground beneath us and the sky above. These two lines form the foundation of everything else.
Earth
A line at the bottom
Sky
A line at the top
Everything in Bliss exists between these two reference lines. Things on earth stay low. Things in the sky reach high.
Lesson 2
The Person
Between earth and sky stands a person. In Bliss, a person is simply a figure standing upright.
Person
A head and body
This symbol appears in many Bliss words. When you see it, you know we're talking about people.
Lesson 3
The Enclosure
A square represents protection, a boundary, something contained.
Enclosure
Protection, building, container
Put a person inside an enclosure and you get... a house! Put food inside and you get... a refrigerator! The combinations are logical.

Lesson 5
Combining Symbols
The power of Bliss is combination. Put simple symbols together and they create new meanings.
Person
+
Enclosure
=
House
A person + an enclosure = a house. The logic is visual. You don't need to memorize—you can figure it out.
Lesson 6
Water and Fire
Natural elements have simple shapes. Water flows in waves. Fire points upward.
Water
Flowing waves
Fire
Flames pointing up
Water under food? Boiling. Fire under food? Cooking. The combinations build meaning from simple parts.
Lesson 7
Heart and Mind
Abstract concepts also have symbols. The heart represents feelings. A shape at the top of the person represents the mind.
Feeling
The heart
Mind
Thoughts rise up
Heart + positive = happy. Mind + question = wondering. Abstract ideas become visual.
Congratulations
You've Started!
You now know the basic building blocks of Bliss Symbolics:
Earth
Sky
Person
Enclosure
Water
Fire
Charles Bliss created this system so people who speak different languages could understand each other. The symbols are logical, not arbitrary. With these basics, you can start to read and build meaning.
